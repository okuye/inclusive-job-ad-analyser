# Example: Biased Job Ad

This is an example of a job description containing multiple types of biased language.

---

## Senior Software Engineer

We're looking for a **rockstar** developer to join our **young and energetic** team! 
Must be a **digital native** who can work in a **fast-paced** environment.

### Requirements:
- **Recent graduate** or 2-5 years experience
- **Aggressive** problem solver who can **dominate** technical challenges
- Must be **able to stand for long periods**
- Join our **bro culture** and **work hard, play hard** mentality
- **Beer o'clock** every Friday!
- **Ivy League preferred**
- Must be a **ninja** at debugging

### About Us:
We're a **young** startup with **crazy good** growth. Looking for someone who can **crush it** under pressure and isn't **deaf to criticism**.

### What We Offer:
- **Competitive** environment
- Office **ping pong table**
- Team of **guys** who love what they do

---

## Issues in this ad:

### Gender-Coded Language (Masculine):
- "rockstar" - exclusionary, masculine-coded
- "ninja" - culturally appropriative, masculine-coded  
- "aggressive" - masculine-coded trait
- "dominate" - aggressive, masculine language
- "crush it" - aggressive language
- "competitive" (environment) - masculine-leaning
- "guys" - male default language

### Ageist Language:
- "young and energetic" - explicit age discrimination
- "digital native" - implies older workers lack tech skills
- "recent graduate" - age-restrictive
- "fast-paced" - can imply age preference
- "young" (startup) - age emphasis

### Ableist Language:
- "able to stand for long periods" - excludes disabilities
- "crazy good" - stigmatizes mental health
- "deaf to criticism" - ableist metaphor

### Culture-Fit Bias:
- "bro culture" - explicitly exclusionary
- "work hard, play hard" - implies drinking culture
- "beer o'clock" - alcohol-centric, exclusionary

### Socioeconomic Bias:
- "Ivy League preferred" - class-based discrimination

**Expected Score:** Poor (30-50/100)
